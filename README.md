### How to Conduct Research

#### Research Workflow

1. **Collection Phase**
   - Gather evidence and source materials
   - Archive URLs and web content
   - Document sources with proper citations
   - Organize materials in appropriate folders

2. **Verification Phase**
   - Validate source reliability
   - Cross-reference claims across sources
   - Fact-check specific statements
   - Assess confidence levels

3. **Analysis Phase**
   - Analyze technical capabilities
   - Map networks and connections
   - Develop timelines
   - Assess threat levels

4. **Documentation Phase**
   - Document findings with evidence
   - Create and update profiles
   - Write research logs
   - Track hypotheses and questions

5. **Review Phase**
   - Review findings for accuracy
   - Verify legal compliance
   - Update conclusions
   - Identify gaps for further research

#### Evidence Collection

**Naming Convention:** `YYYY-MM-DD_[type]_[description].[ext]`

Examples:
- `2020-12-15_screenshot_naomi_seibt_tweet.png`
- `2024-06-20_document_hbo_bitcoin_documentary.pdf`
- `2022-11-30_video_deepfake_example.mp4`

**Documentation Requirements:**
- Timestamp of collection
- Source attribution
- Context and relevance notes
- Archive URL if applicable

#### Source Management

**Source Quality Tiers:**

**Tier 1 (Highest Reliability):**
- Peer-reviewed academic papers
- Official government documents
- Reputable news organizations with editorial standards
- Court documents and legal filings

**Tier 2 (Moderate Reliability):**
- Established news websites
- Expert blogs with credentials
- Industry reports
- Academic conference proceedings

**Tier 3 (Use with Caution):**
- Social media posts
- Unverified online content
- Anonymous sources
- Opinion pieces without factual basis

**Archiving Services:**
- Internet Archive (Wayback Machine): https://web.archive.org/
- Archive.today: https://archive.today/
- Perma.cc: https://perma.cc/

#### Legal and Ethical Guidelines

**What to Include:**
- Publicly available information
- Professional biographical details
- Public statements and publications
- Official documents and filings

**What to Exclude:**
- Private addresses and phone numbers
- Private email addresses
- Financial information
- Medical information
- Family details not publicly disclosed
- Private communications

**Legal Compliance:**
- Data Protection Laws (GDPR, CCPA, privacy regulations)
- Copyright Laws (fair use, attribution, licensing)
- Defamation Laws (avoiding false statements)
- Harassment Laws (avoiding targeted harassment)
- Computer Fraud Laws (legal information gathering)

**Ethical Guidelines:**

**Do:**
- Use only publicly available information
- Verify sources before citing
- Present findings accurately
- Acknowledge uncertainty and limitations
- Respect privacy rights
- Avoid harm to individuals

**Don't:**
- Speculate without evidence
- Make false or defamatory statements
- Harass or stalk individuals
- Invade privacy
- Manipulate or fabricate evidence
- Target individuals for harassment

For detailed research guidelines, see the [Research Folder Structure](./AGENTS.md#research-folder-structure) section in AGENTS.md.
